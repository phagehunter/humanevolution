A field guide to the human family

Twenty-four
cousins.— and only one of them is still walking.

For nearly seven million years, Earth has hosted a wildly diverse family of upright apes. Twenty-three of them are gone. One remains. Below: every known hominin species, plotted, compared, and made to talk to each other. Drag the time slider, scroll the bone-yard, pick two cousins and see how they differ.

03The brain story

The skull tripled in size —
but not in a straight line.

Plot every fossil's cranial capacity against its age and the trend is unmistakable: bigger brains, later in time. But the dataset also remembers the strange ones — Floresiensis the Hobbit, Naledi with its grapefruit-sized brain — small-skulled humans who survived deep into the Pleistocene.
